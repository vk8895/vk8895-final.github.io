The Basics


Short-Term Stock Trading consists of buying and selling stocks either within the same day or within a few days/weeks time. This is to take advantage of short-term changes to the market to pocket additional returns. This is done professionally on Wall Street, but also can be done by individuals at home.

Short-Term Stock Trading is different from Long-Term Investing in that different factors impact the stock price. While federal interest rates, management, yearly cash-flow etc. impact long-term prices, they rarely affect day-to-day trends in the market. There are thousands of other "Technical Indicators" (features) that impact short-term stock prices. The table below summarizes a few of the features that have a greater impact on short-term stock prices:

# Feature Description Potential Impact on Price
1 Timing Outlier Days/Quarters Are there certain days/quarters with high/low prices?
2 Consumer Index Market Sentiment How does public desire for consumption impact stock prices?
3 Volume #Shares Traded Do stocks with larger volume of shares trade at higher prices?
4 Banding Compare 1-week to 2-month trend Are there chances to exploit short-term spikes in prices?
5 Volatility Measuring daily stock bounce range Do stocks that bounce more also close higher?
